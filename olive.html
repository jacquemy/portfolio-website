What is the Olive App?

The Olive app allows users to have access to a variety of types of exercises for mental and physical health such as yoga, workout and meditation classes. The user can find health recipes to help change their eating habits, also the app helps the user to create good habits and change their lifestyle.

Problem Statement

People with no time for “work-life balance” need a way to stay on top of wellness and health needs in a fast, simple and intuitive way because they need to find the time for their mental and psychological health without overwhelming themselves and find a life balance again.

We will know this to be true when we see busy people starting a new routine, creating new habits, prioritising their health and wellbeing and little by little getting back a balanced lifestyle with help from the app.

Potential Solutions

Create an app that includes different modules and services so the user can optimise their time and get new habits, for example, variety of exercises, from yoga to workout at different levels of difficulty with no need for equipment or accessories, healthy recipes with quick preparation and features to help the user to create new habits.

This way the user will have multiple services included in only one app and don’t need to use different apps for different services, helping them to be organised, gain time and able to track their goals easier to keep motivated.

Usability Test

The 6 participants were to complete 4 tasks of the main features of Olive App. After completion of the tasks, the findings and insights were then inserted into a Rainbow Spreadsheet.

From reviewing the data and errors found, we were then able to make improvements and adjustments to have a better user experience in Olive App.

A/B Preference Test

One of the behaviours observed during usability testing has been users to swipe left to get more information about the app in the onboarding.

There were really relevant comments that we took into consideration to make a second test. We kept the picture that users liked most for the Welcome screen of the onboarding and used the second picture for the other part of the onboarding related to exercises. We also changed the design according to the users comments and introduced a second test.
